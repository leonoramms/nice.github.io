About NICE

NICE is the National Instrument Centre for CERN in Denmark. The purpose of NICE is to support and promote the utilisation of CERN and its accelerator, technical and scientific infrastructures by Danish researchers, students and others, and to make it possible for them to participate in experiments and R&D at CERN.

Members and Organisation

About 200 researchers and graduate students in Denmark use CERN either by contributing and participating directly in the experiments or by carrying out theoretical work related to CERN physics.
⚡

Funding

The Danish membership of CERN is covered at the national level by the Danish Ministry of Higher Education and Science. The membership terms are stipulated by international convention and the membership contribution is calculated on the basis of the national GDP.

Industry and knowledge transfer

Technology and CERN go hand in hand. A project like the Large Hadron Collider could only be built based on a remarkable deployment of cutting edge technology in CERN and in the member states.

Outreach and education

Danish high school classes visit CERN every year in large numbers: In recent years, about 900 Danish high school students have visited CERN either by contacting the CERN visits office or by contacting NICE researchers.
🌐

CERN

CERN is an acronym for Centre Européen pour la Recherche Nucleaire. It is the world’s foremost laboratory for fundamental research in high-energy physics and for accelerator development and related advanced technologies.
