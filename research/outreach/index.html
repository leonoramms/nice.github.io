Outreach and education

The CERN Summer Student Programme offers undergraduate students of physics, computing and engineering a unique opportunity to join in the day-to-day work of research teams participating in experiments at CERN in Geneva, Switzerland.

Beyond the outstanding first-class scientific value of their stay, the selected students will find working in a multidisciplinary and multicultural environment an extremely enriching personal experience. It is a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to make valuable, long-lasting contacts with other students and scientists from across Europe.

Visit CERN:

DanishHigh school classes visit CERN every year in large numbers: In recent years about 900 Danish high school students have visited CERN either by contacting the CERN visits office or by contacting NICE researchers.

High School classes can propose an experiment at CERN:

Propose an experiment at CERN and carry it out with real high-energy beam.

NICE encourages Danish High School classes and their teachers to develop proposals. NICE will evaluate proposals that don’t make it to the final at CERN and award a prize for the best project proposal.
