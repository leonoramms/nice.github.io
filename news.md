## Latest News

Stay up to date with discoveries, publications, and events from the world of particle physics
and from the NICE community.

---

<ul class="news-list">

  <li class="news-item">
    <div class="news-date">February 2026</div>
    <h3>LHC Run 3 continues to deliver record luminosity</h3>
    <p>The Large Hadron Collider has exceeded its Run 3 luminosity targets ahead of schedule,
    giving ATLAS and CMS unprecedented datasets for rare process searches. Danish groups are
    actively involved in the analysis of the new data.</p>
    <a href="https://home.cern/news" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Read more at CERN →</a>
  </li>

  <li class="news-item">
    <div class="news-date">January 2026</div>
    <h3>New ALICE FoCal detector modules delivered</h3>
    <p>The first production modules of the ALICE Forward Calorimeter (FoCal), to which Danish
    groups have made significant hardware contributions, have been delivered to CERN for
    integration testing.</p>
  </li>

  <li class="news-item">
    <div class="news-date">December 2025</div>
    <h3>NICE Annual Symposium 2025 — Summary</h3>
    <p>The NICE Annual Symposium 2025 was held in Aarhus with over 80 participants from all
    member institutions. Highlights included presentations on HL-LHC upgrade progress,
    industry partnership news, and a special lecture on the future of particle physics.</p>
    <a href="/meetings/">View meeting minutes →</a>
  </li>

  <li class="news-item">
    <div class="news-date">November 2025</div>
    <h3>Danish team wins best poster at TIPP2025 conference</h3>
    <p>Researchers from the Technical University of Denmark were awarded best poster for their
    work on radiation-hard silicon strip sensor characterisation at the Technology and
    Instrumentation in Particle Physics (TIPP) conference.</p>
  </li>

  <li class="news-item">
    <div class="news-date">October 2025</div>
    <h3>ATLAS publishes new Higgs measurement with full Run 3 data</h3>
    <p>The ATLAS collaboration has published a new precision measurement of Higgs boson
    couplings using the full Run 3 dataset. Danish physicists contributed to the analysis
    of the H→ZZ*→4ℓ decay channel.</p>
    <a href="https://atlas.cern/updates/physics-briefing" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Read ATLAS briefing →</a>
  </li>

  <li class="news-item">
    <div class="news-date">September 2025</div>
    <h3>CERN FCC study group publishes conceptual design update</h3>
    <p>The Future Circular Collider (FCC) study group has published an updated conceptual
    design report for the FCC-ee (electron-positron) stage. NICE is monitoring R&amp;D
    priorities for potential Danish contributions to FCC detector projects.</p>
    <a href="https://fcc.web.cern.ch" target="_blank" rel="noopener">FCC website →</a>
  </li>

  <li class="news-item">
    <div class="news-date">August 2025</div>
    <h3>Danish student wins CERN Summer Student award</h3>
    <p>A master's student from the University of Copenhagen received the best project award
    in the CERN Summer Student Programme for work on machine-learning-based trigger algorithms
    for the ATLAS High-Level Trigger.</p>
  </li>

  <li class="news-item">
    <div class="news-date">June 2025</div>
    <h3>New funding secured for HL-LHC strip tracker work</h3>
    <p>NICE member institutions have secured funding from the Independent Research Fund Denmark
    for continued hardware work on the ATLAS Inner Tracker strip detector, covering production
    and quality control through to installation.</p>
  </li>

</ul>
